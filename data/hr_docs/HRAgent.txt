HUMAN RESOURCES FAQ - MID-SIZED SAAS COMPANY

ONBOARDING & NEW EMPLOYEES
Q: What should I expect on my first day?
A: Your first day includes: orientation session (9:00 AM), IT setup and equipment distribution, introduction to your team, completion of required paperwork, and a welcome lunch. You'll receive a detailed first-day schedule via email the Friday before your start date.

Q: What documents do I need to bring on my first day?
A: Bring: government-issued ID (driver's license or passport), Social Security card or passport, completed I-9 form, direct deposit information (bank account and routing number), emergency contact information, and any required certifications or licenses for your role.

Q: How do I set up my employee account and access systems?
A: You'll receive an email with login credentials on your first day. IT will help you set up your computer, email, and access to company systems. If you don't receive the email, contact IT support or your onboarding buddy.

Q: Who is my onboarding buddy?
A: Your onboarding buddy is an experienced employee who will help you navigate your first few weeks. You'll be introduced on your first day. They can answer questions about company culture, processes, and day-to-day operations.

BENEFITS & INSURANCE
Q: When do my benefits start?
A: Benefits begin on your first day of employment. You have 30 days from your start date to enroll in health, dental, and vision insurance. Life insurance and disability insurance are effective immediately upon enrollment.

Q: What health insurance options are available?
A: We offer three health insurance plans: HMO (lower premium, network restrictions), PPO (higher premium, more flexibility), and High-Deductible Health Plan with HSA (lowest premium, tax-advantaged savings). Detailed plan information is available in the benefits portal.

Q: How do I enroll in benefits?
A: Enroll through the HR portal: HR > Benefits > Enrollment. You have 30 days from your start date to make selections. Changes can be made during open enrollment (typically November) or with a qualifying life event (marriage, birth, etc.).

Q: What's the company's contribution to health insurance?
A: The company covers 80% of health insurance premiums for employees and 60% for dependents. Your portion is deducted pre-tax from your paycheck. Contribution amounts vary by plan selection.

Q: Do you offer dental and vision insurance?
A: Yes, we offer dental (preventive care covered 100%, basic procedures 80%, major procedures 50%) and vision insurance (annual eye exam covered, frames/lenses with copay). Both are optional and available during enrollment.

Q: What retirement benefits are available?
A: We offer a 401(k) plan with company matching. The company matches 50% of your contributions up to 6% of your salary (maximum 3% company match). You're eligible to enroll after 90 days of employment. Vesting is 100% after 3 years.

Q: Do you offer life insurance and disability insurance?
A: Yes, we provide basic life insurance (1x annual salary) and long-term disability insurance at no cost to you. Additional coverage can be purchased during enrollment. Short-term disability is available as an optional benefit.

PAID TIME OFF & LEAVE
Q: How much PTO do I get?
A: PTO accrual varies by tenure: 0-2 years (15 days/year), 3-5 years (20 days/year), 6+ years (25 days/year). PTO accrues monthly and can be used for vacation, personal time, or sick leave. Unused PTO can be carried over up to 5 days.

Q: How do I request time off?
A: Submit time off requests through the HR portal: HR > Time Off > Request Time Off. Include dates, type of leave, and reason. Requests require manager approval. Submit at least 2 weeks in advance for planned time off.

Q: What's the sick leave policy?
A: Sick leave is included in your PTO balance. For extended illness (3+ consecutive days), you may be eligible for short-term disability. Doctor's notes may be required for absences over 3 days. Contact HR for FMLA or disability leave information.

Q: Do you offer parental leave?
A: Yes, we offer 12 weeks of paid parental leave for birth, adoption, or foster placement. Leave can be taken within 12 months of the child's arrival. Additional unpaid leave may be available under FMLA. Contact HR to discuss your situation.

Q: What's the bereavement leave policy?
A: We provide 3-5 days of paid bereavement leave for the death of immediate family members (spouse, children, parents, siblings). Additional unpaid leave may be available. Contact HR to discuss your specific situation.

WORK SCHEDULE & REMOTE WORK
Q: What are the standard working hours?
A: Standard business hours are Monday-Friday, 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM EST, with a 1-hour lunch break. Flexible schedules and remote work options are available based on role and manager approval. Core hours (10 AM - 3 PM) require availability for team collaboration.

Q: Can I work remotely?
A: Remote work eligibility depends on your role. Many positions support hybrid or fully remote work. Discuss remote work options with your manager during onboarding. Remote work agreements must be signed and reviewed annually.

Q: What's the policy on flexible work schedules?
A: Flexible schedules are available with manager approval. Options include: compressed workweeks (4x10 hours), flexible start/end times, and part-time arrangements. All schedules must ensure coverage during core business hours and meet job requirements.

COMPENSATION & PAYROLL
Q: When is payday?
A: Salaried employees are paid on the 15th and last day of each month. Hourly employees are paid bi-weekly on Fridays. Direct deposit is available and recommended. You'll receive a pay stub via email 2 days before payday.

Q: How do I update my direct deposit information?
A: Update direct deposit in the HR portal: HR > Payroll > Direct Deposit. Changes must be submitted by the 1st of the month to take effect for that month's payroll. You'll receive a confirmation email once processed.

Q: How do I access my pay stubs and tax forms?
A: Pay stubs are available in the HR portal: HR > Payroll > Pay Stubs. W-2 forms are available by January 31st each year in the same location. You can download and print pay stubs and tax forms as needed.

Q: When are annual performance reviews and raises?
A: Performance reviews are conducted annually in Q4, with raises effective January 1st. Mid-year check-ins occur in Q2. Raises are based on performance, market data, and company financials. Your manager will discuss your review and compensation.

DEVELOPMENT & TRAINING
Q: What professional development opportunities are available?
A: We offer: internal training programs, conference attendance (with manager approval), online course reimbursement (up to $1,000/year), mentorship programs, and tuition reimbursement (up to $5,250/year). See HR > Development for available programs.

Q: How do I request training or conference attendance?
A: Submit a training request through HR > Development > Training Request. Include: program name, cost, dates, business justification, and how it relates to your role. Requests require manager approval and are subject to budget availability.

Q: Do you offer tuition reimbursement?
A: Yes, we offer tuition reimbursement up to $5,250 per year for job-related courses and degree programs. Courses must be pre-approved by your manager and HR. Reimbursement requires a grade of B or better. Contact HR for the reimbursement form.

PERFORMANCE & FEEDBACK
Q: How does the performance review process work?
A: Performance reviews are conducted annually with mid-year check-ins. The process includes: self-assessment, manager review, goal setting, and development planning. Reviews focus on achievements, areas for growth, and career development.

Q: How do I set goals and track my performance?
A: Goals are set during your annual review and can be updated throughout the year. Track progress in the performance management system: HR > Performance > My Goals. Discuss goal adjustments with your manager as needed.

Q: How do I provide feedback to my manager or HR?
A: Provide feedback through: regular 1-on-1s with your manager, anonymous feedback surveys (quarterly), HR office hours (first Friday of each month), or direct contact with HR. We encourage open and honest feedback.

POLICIES & PROCEDURES
Q: Where can I find the employee handbook?
A: The employee handbook is available in the HR portal: HR > Policies > Employee Handbook. It covers: code of conduct, policies, procedures, benefits, and company culture. You'll receive access during onboarding.

Q: What's the dress code policy?
A: Our dress code is business casual. Appropriate attire includes: collared shirts, slacks or khakis, closed-toe shoes, and professional appearance. Client-facing roles may require business formal. See the Employee Handbook for detailed guidelines.

Q: What's the social media policy?
A: Employees are expected to use social media responsibly. Don't share confidential information, disparage the company or colleagues, or imply you're speaking on behalf of the company. Personal social media use during work hours should be minimal. See the Employee Handbook for the complete policy.

Q: What's the policy on conflicts of interest?
A: Employees must disclose any potential conflicts of interest to HR. Conflicts include: financial interests in competitors, outside employment, family relationships with vendors/clients, and personal relationships that could affect business decisions. Disclosure forms are available in HR > Policies.

LEAVE OF ABSENCE
Q: How do I request a leave of absence?
A: Contact HR to discuss leave options. Submit a leave request form (HR > Leave of Absence > Request Leave) at least 30 days in advance when possible. Include: type of leave, dates, and supporting documentation. HR will guide you through the process.

Q: What types of leave are available?
A: Available leave types include: FMLA (up to 12 weeks for qualifying reasons), short-term disability, long-term disability, personal leave (unpaid, with manager approval), and sabbatical (after 5 years of service). Contact HR to discuss eligibility.

Q: Will my benefits continue during leave?
A: Benefits continue during paid leave. During unpaid leave, you may be responsible for your portion of benefit premiums. COBRA may apply for extended unpaid leave. HR will provide detailed information based on your specific leave type.

TERMINATION & RESIGNATION
Q: How do I resign from my position?
A: Submit a written resignation to your manager and HR. Provide at least 2 weeks' notice (more for senior roles). Include your last day of work. HR will schedule an exit interview and guide you through the offboarding process.

Q: What happens during the exit process?
A: The exit process includes: return of company equipment, access revocation, final paycheck processing, COBRA information (if applicable), and exit interview. HR will provide a checklist and timeline. Your final paycheck will be issued according to state law.

Q: Can I use my remaining PTO after giving notice?
A: Remaining PTO can be used during your notice period with manager approval. Unused PTO is paid out according to state law and company policy. Contact HR to understand your specific payout eligibility.

EMPLOYEE RESOURCES
Q: Do you offer an Employee Assistance Program (EAP)?
A: Yes, we offer a free, confidential EAP that provides: counseling services, legal and financial consultations, work-life balance resources, and crisis support. Access the EAP 24/7 at 1-800-XXX-XXXX or through the HR portal.

Q: What wellness programs are available?
A: We offer: gym membership reimbursement (up to $50/month), wellness challenges, mental health resources, ergonomic assessments, and health screenings. See HR > Wellness for program details and enrollment.

Q: Do you have an employee referral program?
A: Yes, we offer referral bonuses: $1,000 for technical roles, $500 for other roles. Referrals must be hired and remain employed for 90 days. Submit referrals through HR > Referrals. Bonus is paid after the 90-day period.

CONTACT & SUPPORT
Q: How do I contact HR?
A: Contact HR through: email hr@ourcompany.com, HR portal (HR > Contact HR), phone 1-800-XXX-XXXX (Monday-Friday, 9 AM - 5 PM EST), or in-person office hours (first Friday of each month, 2-4 PM). For urgent matters, mark your inquiry as "Urgent."

Q: Who is my HR representative?
A: Your HR representative is assigned based on your department. You'll be introduced during onboarding. You can also find your HR rep in the HR portal: HR > My Information > HR Representative. All HR team members can assist with general inquiries.

Q: What if I have a complaint or concern?
A: Report concerns through: your manager, HR directly, anonymous hotline (1-800-XXX-XXXX), or email concerns@ourcompany.com. All reports are taken seriously and investigated promptly. We have a strict non-retaliation policy.

Q: How do I update my personal information?
A: Update personal information in the HR portal: HR > My Information > Personal Details. You can update: address, phone number, emergency contacts, and personal email. Name changes require documentation and HR assistance.
